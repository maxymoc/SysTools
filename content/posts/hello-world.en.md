I never felt the need to talk about myself.

Twenty years of building things, solving problems, designing systems, without ever thinking about writing it down somewhere. The work spoke for itself, or so I thought.

Then came the pandemic. Then geopolitics. Then those strange, intense years when everything seemed to accelerate and stop at the same time.

At some point you look around and realize something has changed. Not outside, but inside. The way you see things. Priorities. What's worth doing and what no longer is.

One of those realizations is simple and a little uncomfortable: **the sysadmin, in its classic sense, is increasingly a mythological figure**. The context has changed, perimeters have dissolved, competencies have hybridized. Those who haven't noticed risk guarding a boundary that no longer exists.

This blog has no grand ambitions.

It's a notebook. Notes, reflections, technical insights at all levels, written when I have something to say, not according to an editorial plan.

I don't know how consistently I'll manage to keep it up. But today it starts like this, allowing me to learn something new and share what I know.

Ad majora.

Massimo
