## VMUG IT Community Day – Verona, May 27

I waited a few days before writing these lines because I had an important deadline to complete, and as often happens in our work, operational priorities come first.

Now, however, I'd like to pause for a moment, share a few thoughts, and offer my thanks.

You might almost say: "Just the usual story of the usual event?"
**I'd say no.**

When the technical level is high and the community is made up of professionals who tackle these issues in the field every day, the event stops being just a day of sessions and becomes a real discussion. 💻
Meeting professionals in person who you normally follow, read about, or come across online is incredibly valuable to me.
No filters: we talk about real cases, real problems, technical choices, and professional paths.
In a word:
## Experience, yes, with a capital E!

This year I also had the pleasure of meeting and spending time with Fabio Storni, Alessandro Tinivelli, and Paolo Valsecchi — milestones and absolute references — but also many others. I won't name them all so as not to turn this post into a list, but the true value of the day was there too: in the informal conversations, in the shared journey there and back, in sharing news, anecdotes, and discoveries.

Content-wise, the quality of the talks was extremely high.
Special mention goes to the keynotes by Joe Baguley, who was fantastic at providing a vision and understanding of the evolutions we face, almost prophetically, and by Oren Penso, with his "Chaos to Control: Harnessing Agents in the Wild," truly remarkable for its concreteness, relevance, and depth.

Choosing the parallel sessions is never easy: I wanted to attend them all. Each track offered concrete insights and clearly demonstrated the speakers' dedication and passion.

I return from Verona with new ideas, some confirmation, and diverse motivation.
Above all, I return with the conviction that communities like **VMUGIT - VMware User Group Italia** have a role that goes beyond the event itself: they allow those working in the field to exchange views without filters, on real experiences, concrete problems, and possible future directions.

Thanks to **VMUGIT - VMware User Group Italia**, to **Broadcom**, and to everyone I spoke with throughout the day.

Special thanks to **Marco Luvisi**, whom I've been stalking all day! 🤣
