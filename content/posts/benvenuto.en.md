## Why this blog

It's not easy to explain what an Enterprise Architect does to someone outside the field.
They don't install servers, don't write code, don't manage tickets.

They design. They decide. And above all — they justify decisions.

This blog was born from a conviction: **architectural choices deserve to be documented and shared**, not buried in PowerPoint presentations that nobody will ever reopen.

---

## What I'll write about

You won't find tutorials on how to install a service or configure a tool.
You'll find technical reasoning on real problems:

- **ADR** — Architecture Decision Records: why I chose X instead of Y
- **Trade-off analysis** — the honest comparison between alternatives
- **Design patterns** — architectures that work (and some that don't)
- **Lessons learned in the field** — unfiltered retrospectives

The common thread is always the same: **an effective architecture is not the most innovative one, but the one that holds up over time**.

## Who I am

I work as an Enterprise Architect for over 20 years, mainly in:

- **Hybrid Cloud & Private Cloud** — hybrid architectures, VMware, workload placement
- **Modern Workplace** — Microsoft 365, Entra ID, digital workspace
- **Security & Identity** — enterprise hardening, identity-first design
- **Business Continuity** — RTO/RPO, multi-site resilience

I operate at the intersection of technology and strategy — my client is not always the CTO, sometimes it's the CFO who needs to understand why a choice costs what it costs.

---

## Who it's for

Anyone who finds themselves having to **design complex systems** with real constraints: limited budgets, strict compliance, heterogeneous teams, legacy to manage.

I don't write for those looking for simple answers — I write for those who know that simple answers often hide complex problems.
